## 1. MINIMAL & CLEAN

---

### 1A — Swiss Precision

```
STYLE: Swiss Precision
VIBE: Structured, grid-obsessed, clinical clarity. Inspired by the International Typographic Style. Every element exists because it earns its place. Information hierarchy is king.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Helvetica Neue or Suisse Intl (tight letter-spacing, -0.02em)
- Body: Same family, lighter weight
- Sizes follow a strict modular scale (1.25 ratio)
- All-caps for labels and section heads, tracked wide (+0.1em)

COLORS:
- Background: Pure white (#FFFFFF)
- Text: Near-black (#111111)
- Accent: One single hue, used sparingly — red (#FF0000) or blue (#0057FF)
- No gradients. Flat color only.

SPACING:
- 8px base grid, rigorously applied
- Generous whitespace — let elements breathe
- Asymmetric layouts with strong left-alignment

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Hairline rules (1px, light gray) to divide sections
- No border-radius anywhere. Sharp corners only.
- No shadows.

MOTION:
- Minimal. Subtle fade-ins on scroll (200ms, ease-out)
- No bouncing, no spring physics
- Hover states: color shift only, no scale

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: A visible grid overlay or baseline grid reference. Content snaps to an invisible but felt structure.
```

### 1B — Japanese Minimalism (Wabi-Sabi Digital)

```
STYLE: Japanese Minimalism
VIBE: Quiet elegance. Asymmetric balance. Imperfection as beauty. Lots of empty space that feels intentional, like a zen garden. The UI should feel like it's breathing.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Zen Kaku Gothic New, Noto Serif JP, or Cormorant Garamond
- Body: Light weight (300-400), generous line-height (1.8)
- Small text, more whitespace — let the page do the talking
- Mixed-weight pairings: thin display + regular body

COLORS:
- Background: Warm off-white (#F5F0EB) or soft warm gray (#E8E4DF)
- Text: Warm charcoal (#3D3A37)
- Accent: Muted earth tone — clay (#C4A882), moss (#7A8B6F), or indigo (#3D5A80)
- No pure black, no pure white

SPACING:
- Extreme asymmetric whitespace
- Content clustered in one area, vast empty space elsewhere
- Vertical rhythm: 12px base unit

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Almost no visible borders
- Very subtle separators if needed (1px, barely visible)
- Slight border-radius (2-4px) — softened, not rounded
- No box shadows. Use whitespace for separation.

MOTION:
- Slow, graceful transitions (400-600ms)
- Ease-in-out curves, never sharp
- Elements fade and drift slightly (translateY 8px)
- Scroll-triggered reveals, one element at a time

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: One piece of visual texture — a subtle paper grain, a single ink-brush decorative stroke, or a haiku-like micro-copy moment.
```

### 1C — Scandinavian Functional

```
STYLE: Scandinavian Functional
VIBE: Warm minimalism. Clean but cozy. Like an IKEA showroom crossed with a hygge cabin. Function-first but with a human touch — rounded edges, warm tones, natural feeling.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, or Outfit
- Body: Same family, regular weight
- Friendly but clean — no serifs, medium x-height
- Size hierarchy is gentle (don't shout)

COLORS:
- Background: Warm white (#FAFAF7) or pale linen (#F2EDE8)
- Text: Dark warm gray (#2D2A26)
- Accent: Muted pastels — dusty blue (#7EA8BE), sage (#A3B18A), blush (#D4A5A5)
- Secondary: Warm wood tones for hover states or backgrounds

SPACING:
- Comfortable, not extreme — 16px base unit
- Consistent padding (24px-32px on cards)
- Content feels centered and balanced

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Medium border-radius (8-12px)
- Soft shadows (0 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.06))
- Borders are rare; use background color shifts instead

MOTION:
- Gentle and functional — 200-300ms
- Scale up slightly on hover (1.02)
- Smooth page transitions
- Nothing flashy

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Rounded, friendly UI components that feel tactile. Buttons that look like you want to press them. Warm accent colors that feel like natural materials.
```

### 1D — Monochrome Stripped

```
STYLE: Monochrome Stripped
VIBE: Black, white, and nothing else. Ruthlessly reduced. Like a black-and-white photograph — the constraint forces you to nail composition, spacing, and type. Every pixel is intentional.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Bebas Neue, Anton, or any high-contrast condensed sans
- Body: Space Mono, JetBrains Mono, or IBM Plex Mono
- Strong size contrast — huge headlines, small body text
- Uppercase headers, sentence-case body

COLORS:
- Background: White (#FFFFFF) OR Black (#000000) — pick one, commit
- Text: The opposite of background
- Accent: None. Zero color. Gray (#888) for secondary text only.
- Hover states: Invert (black becomes white, white becomes black)

SPACING:
- Dramatic — huge margins, tight line-height on headlines
- Content pushed to edges or extreme center
- Section breaks are just empty space (80px+)

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- No border-radius
- Thick borders (2-3px) when used
- No shadows at all

MOTION:
- Hard cuts, not fades
- Hover: instant color inversion
- Scroll: content snaps into place, no drift

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Pure inversion effects. Hover over a card and everything flips. The entire interface is a study in contrast.
```

## 2. BOLD & EXPRESSIVE

---

### 2A — Neon Maximalist

```
STYLE: Neon Maximalist
VIBE: Loud, electric, unapologetic. Inspired by Tokyo signage, rave culture, and early internet energy. This UI demands attention. Not for corporate — for projects that want to feel alive and a little dangerous.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Clash Display, Unbounded, or Space Grotesk (heavy weights)
- Body: Any clean geometric sans at regular weight
- Oversized headlines that break the grid
- Mixed case, sometimes all-caps with extreme tracking

COLORS:
- Background: Deep dark (#0A0A0A) or very dark navy (#0D1117)
- Neon accents: Electric cyan (#00F0FF), hot magenta (#FF00E5), acid green (#39FF14)
- Use 2-3 neon colors, never muted
- Glow effects: box-shadow with neon color at 40-60% opacity, blurred 20-40px

SPACING:
- Tight in some areas, explosive in others
- Overlapping elements are encouraged
- Break the grid intentionally

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Mix of sharp corners and full-round (pill shapes)
- Neon-colored borders (1-2px) that glow
- No subtle shadows — use colored glows instead

MOTION:
- Energetic — 150-250ms, spring easing
- Pulse/breathe animations on key elements (subtle scale oscillation)
- Glitch effects on hover (translateX jitter, brief color channel split)
- Staggered entrance animations

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Glow. Everything important glows. Text-shadow, box-shadow, backdrop glow. The UI feels like it's emitting light.
```

---

### 2B — Memphis Pop

```
STYLE: Memphis Pop
VIBE: Inspired by the Memphis Group (1980s Italian design). Geometric shapes, clashing colors, squiggles and dots. Anti-minimalist. Joyful, chaotic, irreverent. Great for creative tools, portfolios, or anything that shouldn't feel corporate.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Syne, Bungee, or Fredoka One
- Body: Nunito, Quicksand, or Poppins
- Playful sizing — some words bigger than others within the same heading
- Rotated text elements (-3° to 5°)

COLORS:
- Background: Cream (#FFF5E1) or pale yellow (#FFFDE7)
- Primary shapes: Bold primaries — red (#FF3D3D), blue (#2D7DD2), yellow (#FFD23F)
- Accents: Teal (#11CBD7), coral (#FF6B6B), lavender (#A78BFA)
- Black outlines on everything (2-3px)

SPACING:
- Irregular and intentional
- Elements overlap and stack
- Generous padding inside components, tight between them

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Thick black outlines on cards, buttons, inputs
- Mixed shapes: circles, triangles, squiggly lines as decorative elements
- Some border-radius (12px), some sharp corners — inconsistency is the point
- Drop shadows: hard offset (4px 4px 0px #000)

MOTION:
- Bouncy spring physics
- Rotate on hover (2-5°)
- Shapes float and drift in background (slow, continuous)
- Click: satisfying squish (scale 0.95 then 1.05)

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Scattered geometric decorations (circles, triangles, zigzag lines) that float around the UI as background elements. Hard black-outline aesthetic on interactive components.
```

---

### 2C — Brutalist Web

```
STYLE: Brutalist Web
VIBE: Raw, confrontational, deliberately ugly-beautiful. Inspired by brutalist architecture and anti-design web movements. Feels like a statement. The content IS the design. No polish, all substance.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Courier Prime, IBM Plex Mono, or literally Times New Roman (used ironically)
- Body: System default or monospace
- Massive headlines (5-10rem) that fill the viewport width
- No consistent hierarchy — size is used for emphasis, not structure

COLORS:
- Background: White (#FFFFFF) or screaming yellow (#FFFF00) or raw concrete gray (#C4C4C4)
- Text: Black (#000000)
- Accent: One harsh color — red (#FF0000), blue (#0000FF), or green (#00FF00)
- No gradients, no subtlety

SPACING:
- Extreme and inconsistent
- Some elements crammed together, others floating in vast whitespace
- No padding on some components, 80px on others
- Content touches edges

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Thick borders (3-5px solid black)
- No border-radius (or deliberately mismatched)
- Tables with visible borders
- Elements that look like they're from a 1996 website

MOTION:
- Mostly none — static is a feature
- If any: jarring, instant transitions
- Hover: background color floods entire element instantly
- Marquee text (yes, really)

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Raw HTML energy. Default form elements. Visible structure. The UI looks like it was built in Notepad and that's the point.
```

### 2D — Gradient Overload

```
STYLE: Gradient Overload
VIBE: Lush, saturated, modern. Inspired by Stripe, Linear, and the "beautiful SaaS" aesthetic. Mesh gradients, iridescent surfaces, and smooth transitions. Premium but approachable.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Satoshi, General Sans, or Cabinet Grotesk
- Body: Inter or the same family at lighter weight
- Clean, geometric, modern
- Medium-weight headlines, regular body

COLORS:
- Background: Deep dark (#09090B) or dark gradient (dark purple to dark blue)
- Gradient accents: Purple→Blue→Teal (#7C3AED → #3B82F6 → #06B6D4)
- Alternative: Pink→Orange→Yellow (#EC4899 → #F97316 → #EAB308)
- Text on dark: White (#FFFFFF) and medium gray (#A1A1AA)
- Mesh gradients for hero sections and card backgrounds

SPACING:
- Generous and modern — 16px base
- Large section padding (80-120px vertical)
- Cards with comfortable internal padding (32px)

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Medium border-radius (12-16px)
- Subtle borders: 1px with rgba white at 10% opacity
- Layered shadows with color tinting
- Glassmorphic cards (see also style 11A)

MOTION:
- Smooth and premium — 300-400ms
- Gradient animations (slow hue rotation on backgrounds)
- Cards lift on hover (translateY -4px + shadow increase)
- Staggered fade-up on scroll

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Multi-color mesh gradients as backgrounds or card fills. The kind of gradient that has 3-4 color stops and looks like aurora borealis.
```

## 3. RETRO & NOSTALGIC

---

### 3A — Terminal Green (Hacker Aesthetic)

```
STYLE: Terminal Green
VIBE: Green-on-black CRT terminal. Feels like you're in a 1983 computer lab or hacking into NORAD. Monospace everything. The UI is the command line, but prettier.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Everything: Fira Code, JetBrains Mono, or IBM Plex Mono
- No font variation — monospace only
- Blinking cursor effect on focused inputs
- Text appears to "type" itself on load

COLORS:
- Background: True black (#000000) or very dark green-black (#001100)
- Text: Phosphor green (#00FF41) or amber variant (#FFB000)
- Dim text: Dark green (#005500) or dark amber (#664400)
- No other colors except error red (#FF0000)

SPACING:
- Character-grid aligned
- Consistent padding matching character width
- Tight line-height (1.4)
- Content left-aligned, monospaced columns

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- ASCII-art borders (+----|) or simple 1px green lines
- No border-radius
- No shadows (CRTs don't cast shadows)

MOTION:
- CRT flicker effect (very subtle opacity oscillation)
- Text typing animation (typewriter effect)
- Scanline overlay (repeating 2px transparent/semi-transparent lines)
- Screen "turn on" effect on page load (brightness flash)

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: CRT monitor effects — scanlines, subtle screen curve (via border-radius on outermost container), phosphor glow (text-shadow in the accent color), and a barely-perceptible flicker.
```

---

### 3B — Vaporwave / Retrowave

```
STYLE: Vaporwave
VIBE: Aesthetic nostalgia meets ironic futurism. Pink/purple/teal gradients, chrome text, sunset grids, palm trees, and Roman busts optional but encouraged. The internet's fever dream of the 80s/90s.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Monoton, Megrim, or any chrome/outline display font
- Body: VT323, Press Start 2P, or a clean sans
- Chrome/gradient text effects on headlines (background-clip: text)
- Japanese characters as decorative elements

COLORS:
- Background: Dark purple-blue (#1A0A2E) or sunset gradient (pink→orange→purple)
- Primary: Hot pink (#FF6AD5), cyan (#00D4FF)
- Secondary: Warm purple (#C774E8), sunset orange (#FFA07A)
- Grid lines: Teal or pink at low opacity (20-30%)

SPACING:
- Centered, symmetrical layouts
- Generous vertical spacing
- Content floating on expansive backgrounds

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Neon borders (2px, glowing)
- Chrome/metallic effects via gradients on borders
- No sharp corners — everything slightly rounded (8px)
- Drop shadows in accent colors

MOTION:
- Slow drift animations on background elements
- Infinite scroll on grid backgrounds (perspective grid receding to horizon)
- Gentle pulse on neon elements
- VHS tracking glitch on transitions

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: A perspective grid floor receding to a sunset horizon. Chrome gradient text. The whole thing feels like an album cover from a genre that never existed.
```

### 3C — Y2K / Early Web 2.0

```
STYLE: Y2K Millennium
VIBE: 1999-2003 internet optimism. Bubbly, glossy, chrome. When every button had a highlight reflection and every background was a gradient. iPod-era Apple meets Geocities energy.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Tahoma, Trebuchet MS, or Lexend
- Body: Verdana or any humanist sans (these were THE web fonts)
- Smaller sizes than modern conventions (14px body)
- Bold is BOLD — no light weights

COLORS:
- Background: Light gray (#E5E5E5) or light blue (#D6EAF8)
- Buttons: Glossy gradients (top highlight → color → darker bottom)
- Primary: That specific 2000s blue (#1E90FF), green (#32CD32), or orange (#FF8C00)
- Chrome/silver UI elements (#C0C0C0 → #E0E0E0 gradients)

SPACING:
- Tighter than modern — 4px-8px padding
- Small margins
- Dense information layouts (tables, sidebars, lots of links)

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Rounded corners (6-10px) with visible borders
- Glossy button effect: linear-gradient(180deg, rgba(255,255,255,0.4) 0%, transparent 50%)
- Inset shadows on input fields (pressed-in look)
- Beveled edges on interactive elements

MOTION:
- Almost none (period-accurate)
- Hover: simple highlight/glow
- Maybe a subtle shine sweep across buttons

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: The glossy button. Every button has that telltale top-to-bottom gradient with a white highlight along the top edge. Combined with pill shapes and chrome accents, it screams 2003.
```

### 3D — 8-Bit / Pixel Art

```
STYLE: 8-Bit Pixel
VIBE: NES/Game Boy era. Pixel-perfect graphics, limited color palettes, chiptune energy. Everything looks like it could be a game menu screen. Fun, nostalgic, immediately recognizable.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Everything: Press Start 2P, Silkscreen, or Pixelify Sans
- Pixel fonts only — no anti-aliasing (image-rendering: pixelated)
- Small sizes with big impact
- ALL CAPS for headers

COLORS:
- Use a classic palette constraint — pick 4-6 colors max:
  - NES palette: #000000, #FCFCFC, #F83800, #0058F8, #00A800, #FCA044
  - Game Boy: #0F380F, #306230, #8BAC0F, #9BBC0F (four greens only)
  - SNES pastel: softer versions of the above
- No gradients (unless dithered)

SPACING:
- Grid-locked to multiples of 4px or 8px
- Pixel-perfect alignment
- Tight, compact layouts

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- No border-radius (pixels don't curve)
- 2-4px solid borders
- Box-shadow: 4px 4px 0px #000 (hard pixel shadow)
- Dithered patterns for backgrounds (checkerboard, striped)

MOTION:
- Frame-by-frame feel (use steps() easing, not smooth)
- Sprite-sheet style animations
- Blink effects (opacity toggling)
- Screen shake on error states

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Everything renders on a pixel grid. Corners are stepped, not curved. Shadows are hard-offset blocks. The entire UI feels like it runs on a 256x224 display.
```

### 3E — 90s Operating System (Windows 95/Classic Mac)

```
STYLE: 90s OS
VIBE: Windows 95 meets classic Mac OS. Title bars, beveled buttons, system fonts, nested windows. The aesthetic of an era when UI had TEXTURE — raised panels, sunken inputs, 3D bevels on everything.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Everything: W95FA, Chicago (Mac), or MS Sans Serif — pixel bitmap fonts
- If those aren't available: Segoe UI or Geneva at small sizes (11-13px)
- No decorative fonts. System fonts only.

COLORS:
- Background: That specific Windows gray (#C0C0C0) or Mac platinum (#DDDDDD)
- Title bar: Navy blue (#000080) with white text
- Buttons: Same gray with highlight/shadow bevels
- Active elements: Classic blue (#0000FF) or system highlight
- Backgrounds for content areas: White (#FFFFFF)

SPACING:
- Compact and efficient
- 2-4px padding inside buttons
- 2px gaps between elements
- Title bars: 18-22px height

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- THE classic bevel:
  - Raised: border-top/left 2px solid #FFFFFF, border-bottom/right 2px solid #808080
  - Sunken (inputs): opposite
- No border-radius (0px everywhere)
- Nested panels within panels
- Window chrome: title bar + close/minimize/maximize buttons

MOTION:
- None. Static UI.
- Drag-to-move on windows only
- Selection highlight on click

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Working window chrome — draggable title bars, minimize/maximize/close buttons, resize handles. The entire app looks like it's running inside a Windows 95 desktop.
```

## 4. EDITORIAL & TYPOGRAPHIC

---

### 4A — Newspaper Broadsheet

```
STYLE: Newspaper Broadsheet
VIBE: Classic newspaper layout. Multi-column grids, serif headlines, ruled lines between sections. Authoritative, structured, information-dense. Like reading the front page of a 1970s broadsheet.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Playfair Display, Lora, or Libre Baskerville (bold, large)
- Body: Source Serif Pro, Merriweather, or Charter (regular, readable)
- Strong contrast between headline size (3-4rem) and body (1rem)
- Bylines and datelines in small caps, tracked

COLORS:
- Background: Newsprint off-white (#F5F1EB) or actual white
- Text: Near-black (#1A1A1A)
- Accent: Just red (#CC0000) for breaking/important labels
- Links: Dark blue (#1A3C5E), underlined
- No colored backgrounds. No cards with fills.

SPACING:
- Multi-column layout (CSS columns or grid: 2-4 columns)
- Tight column gaps (20-24px)
- Generous line-height on body (1.6-1.7)
- Minimal padding — content is dense by design

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Horizontal rules between sections (1px solid, dark)
- Vertical rules between columns
- No border-radius
- No shadows
- Pull quotes with large quotation marks

MOTION:
- None. Static. Newspapers don't animate.

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: True multi-column text layout with justified type, drop caps on lead paragraphs, and ruled lines separating stories. Maybe a bold "BREAKING" label in red.
```

---

### 4B — Magazine Editorial

```
STYLE: Magazine Editorial
VIBE: High-end fashion/culture magazine. Full-bleed images, dramatic typography, lots of whitespace punctuated by bold statements. Think Vogue, Kinfolk, or Cereal magazine digitized.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Cormorant Garamond, Bodoni Moda, or Freight Display (light or bold italic)
- Body: Lato, Source Sans Pro, or the display font at regular weight
- Extreme size contrast — display text at 6-10rem, body at 1rem
- Italic for pull quotes and callouts
- Letterspacing on uppercase labels (+0.15em)

COLORS:
- Background: White or very pale neutral (#FAF8F5)
- Text: Black (#000000) or dark charcoal
- Accent: One muted luxury tone — burgundy (#7B2D3F), forest (#2D4A3E), or navy (#1B2838)
- No bright colors. Everything is restrained.

SPACING:
- Extreme whitespace — 120px+ section padding
- Full-viewport-height hero sections
- Asymmetric layouts: image left/text right, or vice versa
- Content rarely centered — pushed to edges

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- No borders, no cards
- Images bleed to edges (no padding, no frames)
- Clean division through whitespace only
- No rounded corners

MOTION:
- Parallax on images (subtle, 10-20% differential)
- Fade-in text on scroll
- Smooth scroll behavior
- Image zoom on hover (scale 1.05, overflow hidden)

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Typography as image. A single word or headline that fills the viewport width. The text IS the visual element.
```

---

### 4C — Monospace Literary

```
STYLE: Monospace Literary
VIBE: A literary journal or indie publisher's website. Everything in monospace, giving it a typewriter/manuscript feeling. Intellectual, slightly academic, deeply readable. Like a beautifully typeset manuscript.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Everything: iA Writer Mono, Courier Prime, or Jet Brains Mono
- Body at comfortable reading size (16-18px)
- Generous line-height (1.75-1.9)
- Indented paragraphs, no space between them (book-style)
- No bold for emphasis — use CAPS or *asterisks* or _underline_

COLORS:
- Background: Warm cream (#FDF6E3) — Solarized light or similar
- Text: Dark sepia (#3B3228)
- Accent: Faded blue (#268BD2) or red (#DC322F) — sparingly
- Link style: underline only, no color change

SPACING:
- Single-column, narrow measure (60-70 characters per line)
- Large side margins pushing content to narrow center column
- Section breaks: three centered asterisks (* * *)

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- No borders
- No cards
- No rounded corners
- Horizontal rules (thin, centered, 40% width) between major sections

MOTION:
- None. The page is a document. It doesn't move.

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: The narrow column, the monospace font, the indented paragraphs — it reads like a manuscript or a Substack post from someone who takes writing very seriously.
```

## 5. DARK & MOODY

---

### 5A — Noir Cinematic

```
STYLE: Noir Cinematic
VIBE: Dark, dramatic, high contrast. Inspired by film noir — deep shadows, narrow shafts of light, desaturated except for one accent color. Feels like a movie poster for a thriller.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Bebas Neue, Oswald, or any tall condensed sans (uppercase)
- Body: Source Sans Pro or Karla (light weight on dark bg)
- Sharp hierarchy — bold headlines, whisper-thin body
- Letterspacing on titles (+0.05em)

COLORS:
- Background: Near-black (#0C0C0C) or very dark charcoal (#151515)
- Text: Off-white (#E0E0E0), never pure white
- Accent: ONE warm color — amber (#D4A843), blood red (#8B1A1A), or gold (#C9A54E)
- Use the accent for highlights, links, and ONE key UI element per screen
- Shadows are deep and warm-tinted

SPACING:
- Cinematic proportions — wide viewport, content centered
- Large vertical padding between sections (100px+)
- Asymmetric image/text pairings

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Minimal borders — use shadow and light instead
- Subtle border-radius (4px)
- Deep, layered shadows (0 10px 40px rgba(0,0,0,0.6))
- Vignette effect on backgrounds (radial gradient from transparent to darker edges)

MOTION:
- Slow, dramatic reveals (500-800ms)
- Fade from darkness
- Ken Burns effect on images (slow zoom and drift)
- Parallax that creates depth layers

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Dramatic lighting simulation. A radial gradient "spotlight" that illuminates the content area while the edges fade to darkness. Combined with the single accent color, the page feels like a scene lit by a single lamp.
```

---

### 5B — Midnight Luxury

```
STYLE: Midnight Luxury
VIBE: The digital version of a high-end cocktail bar at 11pm. Dark, plush, gold accents. Everything whispers "expensive." Great for premium products, fintech, or anything that wants to feel exclusive.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Playfair Display, Cormorant, or Didot
- Body: Outfit, Satoshi, or Manrope (clean contrast to serif display)
- Elegant weight transitions — thin display (300), regular body (400)

VIBE: The future as imagined in 1979. Alien's Nostromo computer, Blade Runner's Voight-Kampff machine, 2001's HAL interface. Amber or green CRTs, chunky physical buttons with beveled labels, lo-fi displays showing hi-fi data. Not the clean CRT of Terminal Green — this has HARDWARE. Bezels, switches, panel labels with embossed text. The computer as a physical object in a spacecraft.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Inconsolata, Overpass Mono, or OCR-A
- Body: Same monospace family
- Strictly monospace throughout
- Chunky, wide characters (letter-spacing: 0.1em)
- All-caps for labels and readouts
- Segment-display style for numbers (or at least tabular-nums)
- Text has a slight CRT bloom (text-shadow in amber/green at 2-3px blur)

COLORS:
- Background: CRT off-black (#0C0A08) with slight warm tint
- Primary option A: Amber (#FFB000) with dark amber (#664400) for secondary
- Primary option B: Green (#33FF33) with dark green (#006600) for secondary
- Bezel/hardware: Dark gray (#2A2A2A), panel gray (#3A3838)
- Label backgrounds: slightly lighter panel (#444442)
- No color mixing — pick amber OR green, commit
- CRT glow: text-shadow in primary color, blurred

SPACING:
- Panel-based: distinct hardware bezels around groups of controls
- Tight internal spacing (8-12px) — CRT screens were small
- Hardware gaps between panels (thicker borders simulating physical separation)
- Everything organized into instrument clusters

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Double/triple borders simulating physical bezels:
  - Outer: 2px solid #444
  - Inner: 1px solid #222
  - Between them: 4px of panel-gray background
- No border-radius (hardware has machined edges)
- Physical button appearance: raised bevels (similar to Win95 but darker)
- Panel labels: small rectangles with embossed text above controls
- Indicator lights: small circles with glow (4-6px, colored)
- Switch/toggle appearance on interactive elements

MOTION:
- CRT power-on: brightness flash then settle
- Characters appear one at a time (typewriter, 30-50ms per char)
- Scanline roll (slow vertical gradient sweep)
- Indicator light blink (1s on, 0.5s off for warnings)
- Screen phosphor fade (text slowly dims when updated, old text ghosts behind new)

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Physical hardware simulation. The UI doesn't just look like a CRT — it looks like a CRT mounted in a PANEL with physical bezels, screws, label plates, and indicator lights around it. The screen is one element within a larger instrument panel. Chunky toggle switches, embossed text labels above controls, and the slight warm amber glow of phosphor displays. Everything feels like you could reach out and flip a physical switch.
```

### 19F — Diesel Deco

```
STYLE: Diesel Deco
VIBE: Art Deco's grittier, heavier sibling. 1930s-40s industrial futurism where streamlined trains and chrome diners meet factory floors and diesel exhaust. The Rocketeer meets Metropolis. Everything Art Deco was — sunbursts, geometry, gold — but heavier, dirtier, more mechanical. Chrome is tarnished. Gold is brass. The future is powered by combustion.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Oswald, Bebas Neue, or Teko (condensed, heavy)
- Body: Barlow or Source Sans Pro
- Condensed display at extreme weights (800-900)
- All-caps with moderate tracking (+0.08em)
- Industrial/stencil variants for labels
- Bold hierarchy: numbers and headlines dominate

COLORS:
- Background: Industrial dark (#1A1714) or gunmetal (#2A2824)
- Primary: Tarnished gold (#B8963E), dirty brass (#A0843A)
- Secondary: Chrome steel (#8A8D8F), industrial red (#993333)
- Accent: Diesel blue (#2A4A6A) or exhaust smoke gray (#6A6A6A)
- Metallic gradients: darker, less shiny than pure Deco (#B8963E → #6A5A2A)
- Rivet/bolt accents in dark steel

SPACING:
- Heavier than Art Deco — everything has mass
- Moderate padding with thick structural borders
- Symmetrical but with industrial weight
- Section dividers are thick bars, not thin lines
- Panels feel riveted together

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Art Deco geometry but HEAVIER: thick sunburst lines, bold chevrons
- Rivet decorations along panel edges (small circles every 20-30px)
- Thick borders (3-4px) in steel colors
- Streamline curves (elongated radius on horizontal elements, like a train nose)
- No delicate filigree — everything is industrial-gauge
- Gear/mechanical decorative elements mixed with Deco sunbursts

MOTION:
- Heavy, mechanical (300-400ms, ease-out)
- Piston-like movements (linear translation, slight pause at endpoints)
- Steam hiss: blur effect on transitions
- Elements slide into place like machinery engaging
- Nothing delicate — everything has weight and momentum

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Deco sunburst patterns rendered in heavier lines with industrial coloring. Rivet lines along panel edges. Streamline curves on horizontal elements (asymmetric border-radius: 0 40px 40px 0). The gold is duller, the chrome is darker, the geometry is thicker. If Art Deco Glamour is the cocktail lounge, Diesel Deco is the engine room of the same building.
```

### 19G — Salvagepunk

```
STYLE: Salvagepunk
VIBE: Post-apocalyptic resourcefulness. Everything is built from scrap, rewired, and jury-rigged from whatever was available. Mismatched panels, exposed wiring, patched-together components from three different design systems. A UI that was assembled from the ruins of civilization and WORKS, dammit. Mad Max's mechanic built this interface.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Mix of 2-3 different fonts deliberately (Archivo Black + VT323 + system serif)
- Body: Whatever's available — different components use different fonts
- Stencil text (Saira Stencil One) for warnings/labels
- Handwritten (Caveat, Patrick Hand) for annotations/patches
- The inconsistency IS the design — nothing matches because nothing was designed together
- Some text scratched out and rewritten (strikethrough + replacement)

COLORS:
- Background: Rust (#3A2A1E) or dirty metal (#2E2E28)
- Primary: Warning amber (#CC8833), hazard orange (#CC6622)
- Secondary: Corroded green (#4A6A3A), old wire copper (#8B5A3A)
- Accent: Spark blue (#4488CC) for "still working" indicators
- Danger: Faded red (#994433)
- Surface: Mismatched panels — each card/section a slightly different shade
- Texture: heavy noise overlay, scratched metal, rust spots

SPACING:
- Uneven and patched — nothing aligns perfectly
- Some panels tight (bolted together), others have gaps (things fell off)
- Duct-tape bridges between sections (decorative connection elements)
- Wire/cable SVG lines connecting distant components
- Dense where critical, sparse where scavenged

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Mismatched border styles (one card has thick borders, the next has thin, the next has dashed)
- Bolt/screw decorations at corners (circles with cross/slot marks)
- Duct tape strips (semi-transparent gray rectangles at angles)
- Welding seam lines (wavy, slightly glowing)
- Some border-radius, some sharp — inconsistency is the point
- Exposed grid lines (the "wiring" of the layout visible)
- Patch marks: elements that look added later (different style from their container)

MOTION:
- Unreliable — some animations smooth, some jittery
- Spark effect on connections (brief flash)
- Flicker on "damaged" elements (occasional opacity dip)
- Mechanical clunk on interaction (slight overshoot then settle)
- Some elements lag or stutter intentionally

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: Visible patchwork. Every component looks like it was scavenged from a different design system and jury-rigged together. One card has sharp corners and a stencil font, the next has rounded corners and a serif. They're connected by duct-tape strips and wire-line SVGs. The UI works, but it clearly wasn't built — it was ASSEMBLED from whatever survived.
```

### 19H — Hauntology

```
STYLE: Hauntology
VIBE: Mark Fisher's concept: the future that never arrived. Faded public information films, BBC Radiophonic Workshop, abandoned modernist architecture, Boards of Canada album covers. The ghost of a utopian future that was promised in the 1970s and never came. Deeply British, deeply melancholic, strangely beautiful. Like finding a pamphlet from 1974 about the world of tomorrow, yellowed and forgotten in a library that's being demolished.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Any 1970s public-information font — Albertus, Optima, or Futura (medium weight)
- Body: Same family or a clean humanist sans (Lato, Source Sans Pro)
- NOT monospace — these are government-designed informational fonts
- Medium weights, comfortable sizes
- Sometimes in all-caps with wide tracking (public signage style)
- Teletext/Ceefax-style blocky text for accent elements

COLORS:
- Background: Faded institutional cream (#E8E0D0) or concrete gray (#C4C0B8)
- Primary: Faded brown-orange (#AA7744), institutional blue (#3A6688)
- Secondary: Muted mustard (#AA9944), faded teal (#5A8A7A)
- All colors DESATURATED and YELLOWED — like a photograph left in the sun for 40 years
- Apply a subtle sepia/yellow tint to everything
- Static/grain overlay at 4-6% opacity over the entire page

SPACING:
- Institutional — organized like a government pamphlet
- Moderate margins, structured grid
- Clear hierarchy but slightly too much space between elements (lost information)
- Section numbers (1., 2., 3.) as structural navigation
- Content feels official but abandoned

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Clean, modernist shapes — rounded rectangles (8px), circles
- 1970s-style ruled lines (1-2px, warm brown)
- Retro-futurist diagrams and arrows
- Teletext-style block graphics (▐█▌ using CSS)
- Tape damage: horizontal line artifacts
- Aged paper texture overlay
- No shadows — flat print aesthetic

MOTION:
- VHS degradation effects (subtle horizontal jitter, color fringing)
- Slow fade between states (400-600ms)
- Static bursts (brief noise overlay flash, rare)
- Film grain: continuous subtle animation
- Elements appear as if being tuned in on an old TV — slight vertical roll then lock

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: The yellowed, degraded quality of everything. Every color is shifted warm and desaturated as if viewed through aged cellophane. A noise/grain overlay covers the entire interface. Combined with 1970s public-information typography and institutional spacing, the UI feels like a transmission from a future that was promised but never built — received on a television that's been sitting in an empty room since 1978.
```

### 19I — Dieselwave

```
STYLE: Dieselwave
VIBE: NOT vaporwave, not synthwave. Dieselwave is the 1930s-40s run through the same nostalgic distortion filter. Art Deco skyscrapers seen through Instagram. Sepia-toned noir with modern glitch effects. Old Hollywood glamour compressed into a lossy format. The Great Gatsby but the video file is corrupted. Analog warmth meeting digital decay.

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Display: Playfair Display, Bodoni Moda, or any high-contrast serif (thin/thick stroke variation)
- Body: Lato or Source Sans Pro
- Elegant serif display with clean sans body
- Chrome/metallic gradient fill on display text (background-clip: text)
- Some text with film-grain texture overlay
- Art Deco tracking on uppercase text (+0.15em)

COLORS:
- Background: Warm dark sepia (#1A1510) or film-noir dark (#0F0D0A)
- Primary: Sepia gold (#C4A060), amber (#B89040)
- Secondary: Film blue-gray (#5A6A7A), silver screen (#A0A0A0)
- Highlight: Warm white (#F0E8D0) — never cool white
- Glitch accents: Cyan (#00CCCC) and magenta (#CC0066) for digital decay moments
- Overall: everything has a sepia filter, but glitch elements break through in synthetic colors

SPACING:
- Cinematic proportions — widescreen feeling
- Generous vertical spacing
- Content centered, classic composition
- Section breaks: film-strip decorations (sprocket holes)

BORDERS & SHAPES:
- Deco geometry: sunbursts, chevrons, fan shapes
- Film strip borders (repeating rectangles along edges)
- Glitch corruption: some elements have pixel-shift artifacts (box-shadow offset in cyan/magenta)
- Clean Deco structure partially degraded
- No border-radius (Deco is angular)
- Scanline overlay (lighter than CRT, more like film grain)

MOTION:
- Film projector flicker (subtle brightness oscillation, 18fps feel using steps())
- Glitch moments: brief color-channel split (translateX offset on pseudo-elements in cyan/magenta)
- Content reveals like a film dissolve (opacity + slight blur clear)
- Sprocket judder: slight vertical bounce
- Mostly slow and cinematic, with rare digital interruptions

DISTINCTIVE ELEMENT: The collision of analog warmth and digital decay. Clean Deco typography and geometry, but with occasional glitch artifacts — a color-channel split, a scanline interruption, a pixel offset. As if a pristine 1930s film was digitized badly and the compression artifacts became part of the aesthetic. Sepia gold meets glitch cyan in moments of beautiful corruption.
```
